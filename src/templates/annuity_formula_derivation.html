Preamble: Geometric Series Formula

Recall the finite geometric series: $$ S_n = a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + ... + ar^{n-1} + ar^n $$ Multiplying the series by \( r \) gives: $$ rS_n = ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + ... + ar^{n} + ar^{n+1} $$ Thus $$ \begin{align*} S_n - rS_n = & \left( a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + ... + ar^{n-1} + ar^n \right) \\ & - \left( ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + ... + ar^{n} + ar^{n+1} \right) \end{align*} $$ Keeping all RHS terms in the same order but moving a couple of brackets: $$ \begin{align*} (1 - r)S_n = a & + \left( ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + ... + ar^n \right) \\ & - \left( ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + ... + ar^{n} \right) - ar^{n+1} \end{align*} $$ The terms in brackets cancel, giving $$ \begin{align*} (1 - r) S_n & = a - ar^{n+1} \\ (1 - r) S_n & = a(1 - r^{n+1}) \end{align*} $$ Finally $$ S_n = a \left( \frac{ 1 - r^{n+1} }{ 1 - r } \right) $$

Annuity Formula

Define:

\(P:\) Principal (amount borrowed)
\(n:\) Loan term in months
\(r:\) The monthly interest rate (as a fraction, not percentage)
\(A:\) Annuity (constant amount paid back each month)

Consider the trivial case, a loan with a term of just one month. Then the amount owed after one month will be \( P(1 + r) \), which will be fully paid off by the single annuity: $$ \mathrm{Term} \; n = 1: \;\;\;\; P(1 + r) - A = 0 $$ For a loan term of two months we have: $$ \begin{align*} \mathrm{Term} \; n = 2: \;\;\;\; \left[ P(1 + r) - A \right](1 + r) - A & = 0 \\ P(1 + r)^2 - A(1 + r) - A & = 0 \end{align*} $$ And for a loan term of three months: $$ \begin{align*} \mathrm{Term} \; n = 3: \;\;\;\; \left[ P(1 + r)^2 - A(1 + r) - A \right](1 + r) - A & = 0 \\ P(1 + r)^3 - A(1 + r)^2 - A(1 + r) - A & = 0 \end{align*} $$ We can now see a general pattern: $$ \mathrm{Term} \; n: \;\;\;\; P(1 + r)^{n} - A(1 + r)^{n-1} ... - A(1 + r)^2 - A(1 + r) - A = 0 $$ Or $$ \mathrm{Term} \; n: \;\;\;\; P(1 + r)^{n} - A \left[ (1 + r)^{n-1} + ... + (1 + r)^2 + (1 + r) + 1 \right] = 0 $$ The term in the square brackets is a finite geometric series. Recall the formula for the sum of a finite geometric series: $$ S_t = a \left( \frac{ 1 - x^{t+1} }{ 1 - x } \right) = ax^t + ax^{t-1} + ... + ax^2 + ax + a $$ Now, an equivalent formula may be more convenient for some purposes (including this one): $$ S_t = a \left( \frac{ (-1)(1 - x^{t+1}) }{ (-1)( 1 - x )} \right) = a \left( \frac{ x^{t+1} - 1 }{ x - 1 } \right) $$ Let \( a = A \), and define \( x = (1 + r) \). Further define \( t = n - 1 \) so that \( t + 1 = n \), and the term in the square brackets becomes: $$ A \left( \frac{ (1 + r)^{n} - 1}{ (1 + r) - 1 } \right) $$ Or $$ A \left( \frac{ (1 + r)^{n} - 1 }{ r } \right) $$ So for a loan of term \( n \): $$ \mathrm{Term} \; n: \;\;\;\; P(1 + r)^{n} - A \left( \frac{ (1 + r)^{n} - 1 }{ r } \right) = 0 $$ Rearranging: $$ P(1 + r)^{n} = A \left( \frac{ (1 + r)^{n} - 1 }{ r } \right) $$ $$ P(1 + r)^{n} \left( \frac{r}{ (1 + r)^{n} - 1 } \right) = A $$ Finally, the Annuity Formula: $$ A = P \frac{r (1 + r)^{n}}{ (1 + r)^{n} - 1 } $$
